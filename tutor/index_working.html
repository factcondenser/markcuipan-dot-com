Teaching Philosophy

Make learning personal.

Unless they’re personally meaningful or immediately applicable, most tidbits of information that enter our brains are promptly forgotten. That’s why, every time that I teach, I make a no-holds-barred effort to ensure that my students are able to connect novel strategies and information to their existing knowledge and experiences.

Practice, practice, practice!

Dedicated practice won’t lead to perfection, but it will guarantee progress.

Hard work doesn't mean no fun.

Your child probably carries around with them a device capable of accessing the sum of human knowledge with just a few finger swipes. Yet, most of the time, they use it to WhatsApp their friends or play ‘Minion’s Paradise’. Why? Because texting Jenny from period 3 chemistry is more fun than reading about municipal bonds on Investopedia. Or so they think.
In my role as an educator, I strongly advocate that learning can and should be fun. Gaining information about the world around them shouldn’t make children miserable. It should enlighten and inspire them. In an economic landscape that changes faster by the hour, it’s now more important than ever to instill in our kids the skills and passion necessary to pursue a lifetime of learning. I do this by helping my students strike a healthy and productive balance between work and play.

What Sets Me Apart

I’m young enough that the advice I give your child will still carry the weight of peer pressure. Think of me as that friend you’d want to see them hanging around with more often. Not only am I an academic powerhouse (I was valedictorian of my high school and made the dean’s list every year while in college), I’m also highly sociable (I was homecoming king as well!). I have a diverse set of interests that allows me to comfortably connect with and influence people – especially kids who aren’t super motivated to study. Here are some topics over which past students and I have bonded: agricultural robotics, Nietzsche, Salvador Dali, viola-hate jokes (I play the viola, so it’s okay…), ultimate frisbee, Harry Potter, and 2Pac.
